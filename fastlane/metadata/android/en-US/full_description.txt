PocketRig turns your Android phone into a small, well-behaved Monero (XMR) miner.
It runs the RandomX algorithm natively (a bundled, from-source build of xmrig) under
a foreground service, so it keeps working with the screen off — but it is built to be
a good citizen of your device, not to cook it.

The point is not profit. Mining on a phone earns pennies and makes heat. PocketRig
exists to do it <i>safely and transparently</i> on hardware you own, for fun and
learning.

<b>Adaptive governor</b>
An on-device governor scales the miner from 1 to all performance cores based on live
signals:
* Screen on? It drops to 1-2 cores so the phone stays snappy.
* Chip warming up? It backs off before it gets hot.
* On battery and draining? It eases off, and pauses entirely near a low-battery floor.
* Plugged in and cool? It uses the whole chip.
The system (prime) cores are reserved so your phone never feels sluggish.

<b>Features</b>
* Pick your own pool (MoneroOcean, SupportXMR, HashVault, Nanopool, 2Miners, or a
  custom host:port) and your own wallet.
* Fixed 2/4/6/8-core modes or fully adaptive.
* Optional auto-start when you plug in the charger (off by default).
* Live hashrate, per-session stats, and a temperature-tinted core ring.
* Scan a wallet QR from the camera or a screenshot.
* Share the app phone-to-phone over local Wi-Fi via a QR code.

<b>Developer fee (disclosed)</b>
For about 40 seconds out of every 30 minutes of mining (~2.2%), hashes go to the
developer's wallet instead of yours. This is the same model xmrig itself uses. It is
shown in the app while it is active and is a permanent, disclosed part of the app.

<b>Privacy</b>
No analytics, no tracking, no ad networks. The UI ships entirely inside the app (no
web fonts, no CDNs) and phones home to nothing except the mining pool you choose. Your
wallet is a public receive address; it is stored only on your device.

This is free software (GPLv3). Source, build instructions, and the developer's donation
address are in the project README.
